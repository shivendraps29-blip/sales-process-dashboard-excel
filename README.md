Multi-sheet Excel sales dashboard covering 5 years of data —  Cash Flow, Working Capital &amp; Sales Transactions tracked  across 8 US cities, 6 income sources &amp; B2B/B2C segments.

# 📊 Sales Process Dashboard | Excel Project

## About This Project

This project was about building a proper multi-sheet sales 
dashboard in Excel that goes beyond just one chart and a 
pivot table. The idea was to track a business's full financial 
picture — income coming in, working capital position, cash 
flow movement, and individual sales transactions — all 
connected through one central dashboard.

The dataset covered 5 years (2021–2025) across 8 US cities, 
with 480 records, 20,360 total orders and $14.09M in 
total income analyzed.

---

## What the Dashboard Covers

The workbook is structured into 4 main sections — 
each one answers a different business question:

**Dashboard** — the central hub that connects everything. 
Navigation links to each section are built in so you can 
jump between sheets without scrolling.

**Cash Flow** — tracks income vs target income month by month, 
showing where the business hit its targets and where it 
fell short across 5 years.

**Working Capital** — monitors operating profit and the 
financial health of the business on a running basis. 
Useful for understanding whether the business has 
enough liquidity at any given point.

**Sales Transactions** — breaks down individual transactions 
by income source, city, market strategy, payment method, 
POS channel and customer registration status.

---

## What I Was Trying to Find Out

- Which income source is generating the most revenue?
- Is the business more B2B or B2C — and by how much?
- Which cities are the strongest markets?
- How has income grown year over year from 2021 to 2025?
- What is the split between Website vs Shop-based sales?
- How does registered vs non-registered customer behavior 
  differ in terms of refund rates?

---

## Key Findings

**Income Sources — Retail leads but Royalty is close**
Retail brought in $4.02M, followed by Royalty at $3.54M 
and Renting at $2.82M. SaaS and Fees were the smallest 
contributors at under $1M combined — interesting 
considering SaaS tends to be a high-margin stream.

**B2B dominates**
B2B accounted for $10.07M vs B2C at $4.02M. 
Nearly 71% of all revenue is business-to-business 
which shapes how the sales strategy should be built.

**San Francisco is the top city**
San Francisco led at $3.06M, followed by Denver ($2.82M) 
and New York ($2.25M). Miami and Chicago were at the 
lower end — possible targets for growth investment.

**Revenue has grown consistently every year**
2021: $1.27M → 2025: $3.85M — that's a 3x growth 
over 5 years. The jump between 2022 and 2023 was 
particularly sharp, going from $2.1M to $3.3M.

**Average Operating Profit per transaction: $4,914**
This varied significantly by income source — 
something worth digging into further by category.

## Structure of the Workbook

6 sheets total:

- **DASHBOARD** — main navigation and summary view
- **Cash Flow** — monthly income vs target tracking
- **Working Capital** — operating profit monitoring
- **Sales Transactions** — transaction-level detail

  feeding the dashboard charts
- **Data** — the raw dataset (480 rows × 16 columns)
